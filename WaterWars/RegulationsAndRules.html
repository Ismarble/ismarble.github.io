🔫 Regulations And Rules

Preface

Water Wars is a competitive, team-based elimination game played using water guns. Matches are active on set days with set timeframes, and all gameplay must occur within that window. Players earn 2 points per elimination, with eliminations in the final two hours of the scheduled day earning 3 points.

Public parks are designated high-risk zones and award double points for eliminations (e.g., 4 points normally, 6 points in the final two hours). See Section 7.1 for definitions of a park.

The judge(s) will make an effort to include at least 25% of team members who are eligible to drive. This is to encourage balanced mobility and fairness between teams.

This rulebook outlines safe zones, conduct expectations, gameplay procedures, penalties, and the authority of the judge(s). All participants are expected to follow these rules in the spirit of fair play and fun.

Team members and teams are encouraged to build their own weapons should they wish; all types of parts are allowed. Be smart when designing your gun, read and obey all rules. (Note Section 3.3 and 3.4)

Store-bought guns are allowed, and do not need to be checked by judges, but must follow every rule.

SECTION 1:

Safe Zones – No Engagement Areas

1.1. Workplaces

  • Players may not engage in any water combat while a target is at their workplace or on break.
  • This rule is in effect from the time the player clocks in at the job site until they clock out.

1.2. Places of Worship

  • No engagements are allowed inside or on the premises of any place of worship, including associated parking lots.
  • Last year, we used church parking lots to confer with other teams; this behavior is encouraged. Please remain respectful of the property.
  • This includes but is not limited to churches, synagogues, mosques, and mandirs.

1.3 Medical Facilities

  • No engagements are allowed inside or on the premises of any medical facility.

1.4 Learning Facilities

  • No engagements are allowed inside or on the premises of any LSR7 facilities, including but not limited to summer camps, the shop, and the parking lot of both.

1.5 Expectations on private property

  • Players must not shoot from or into any No Engagement Areas outlined in any section of the rules.

SECTION 2:

Property and Entry Protocol

2.1. Illegal Access Prohibited

  • Trespassing is strictly forbidden. This includes but is not limited to climbing fences, breaking locks, or forcing entry.
  • Even if a door is left unlocked or open, no player is allowed to enter without completing the Entry Permission Form (see 2.2 Entering a Private Residence)
  • Including Garage Doors, Fence Gates, Front Doors, Back Doors, ETC

2.2. Entering a Private Residence

  • Entry into a residence is only permitted with written consent from a resident who holds legal parental or guardianship responsibilities to the player whose home is being entered. (Entry Permission Form)
  •  
  • Legal kills committed on private property are worth 1.5x points only if using the Entry Permission Form. Applied after any other point modifications (See Preface)
  •  
  • Kills made from inside private property to outside will not count, to discourage camping or other forms of defense
  • In layman's terms, you can't shoot out of your house's windows; you need to come outside to fight.

2.3. Time Limit for Home Entry

  • Players may remain inside a consenting home for a maximum of one hour.
  • Written permission must specify the approximate start and end time of the visit.

2.4. Entry Procedure

  • The person who granted written permission must be the one to allow you entry into the home personally.

SECTION 3:

Gameplay Rules

3.1. No Vehicular Combat

  • All combat involving motorized vehicles is strictly prohibited.
  • Cars, Trucks, Boats, Gokarts, etc.
  • Players may not shoot from or at a moving or stationary motorized vehicle, nor may such vehicles be used to assist in eliminations (Per 3.1.1).

  • These rules are only in effect if the vehicle is used for transportation; vehicles cannot be used as a “safe zone.”
  • All players are expected to remain clear of operating vehicles for safety. This includes attempting to prevent a vehicle from driving.
  • Non-Transportation includes being stopped in a legal parking spot or if the vehicle’s transmission is in park.

3.2. Eliminated Player Conduct

  • Eliminated players are not allowed to communication with any team regarding the whereabouts of other teams. This rule is in place to prevent an eliminated player from acting as a spy.

  • Once a player is eliminated, they may assist their team with planning or support, but may not perform eliminations. Eliminated players are allowed to charge and distribute weapons, but not to fire them in a manner to eliminate. (Should an eliminated player attempt to eliminate you*, 3-hour immunity is gained)

*Only if the eliminated player is attempting to eliminate you, taunting does not count.

3.3. Approved Liquids

  • Only clean, non-harmful water may be used (cool, warm, or room temperature).
  • Water sourced from Pools, Lakes, or Oceans is not permitted due to contaminants such as Chlorine, Salt, Algae, or Bromine
  • The intentional use of any substance other than water, including but not limited to harmful additives, is strictly forbidden. (Note: although we are using clean water, goggles are still wise for strategy)
  • As a rule of thumb, don’t leave water in your water gun overnight; this will make the water stale and yucky, or in the case of metal tanks…. Rusty.

3.4. Weapon Safety

  • All water weapons must be safe for players and may not cause pain or leave visible marks (e.g., red spots, bruises)
  • All homemade or modified weapons must be declared to the judge(s) before that weapon can be used in combat. Judge(s) a judge may not inspect their own weapon, but may inspect, test, and either approve or reject weapons based on safety.
  • Homemade weapons are entirely allowed. Here are some resources to help:
  • DIY Water Gun (Water bottle)
  • Easy $5 Water Guns (PVC Pipe)
  • DIY-Spyra Electric Water Gun (Electric)
  • Garden Hose Water Gun
  • Homemade Shield
  • Manivelle Water Gun
  • Violations of weapon safety guidelines will be reviewed on a case-by-case basis and are subject to penalties at the discretion of the judge(s).

3.5 Player Protection

  • If a player deems it necessary, a temporary combat halt can be called. To call a Combat Halt, you must drop your weapon and form a T with your arms to signify a time-out (Example shown) 
  • All Players must respect the Combat Halt by ceasing fire, lowering their weapons, and spreading the word to all Local Players
  • No eliminations are permitted during a Combat Halt
  • No weapons may be discharged during a Combat Halt
  • Abusing the Player Protection Combat Halt will result in much higher penalties, including possible ejection from the game. The Player Protection Combat Halt is not in place as a tactical timeout.

Valid Combat Halt

Invalid Combat Halt
  • Medical distress (e.g., asthma attack, difficulty breathing, dizziness, fainting)
  • Physical injury (e.g., fall, collision, suspected sprain, bleeding)
  • Eye exposure causing impaired vision (water in eyes that prevents safe movement)
  • Hazardous environment (e.g., moving vehicles nearby, unsafe terrain, bystander interference)
  • Unsafe or non-compliant equipment (e.g., weapon causing pain, suspected contaminated water)
  • Property risk requiring immediate stop (imminent risk of damage to public or private property)
  • Strategic disadvantage (e.g., “my team is losing,” “I’m outnumbered”)
  • Wanting to reposition, retreat, or avoid elimination
  • Running out of water or equipment malfunction
  • Minor discomfort (e.g., tired, out of breath, annoyed)
  • Clothing or gear adjustments (e.g., shoe untied, fixing outfit)
  • Desire to pause for planning or communication
  • Claiming a rule violation without clear, immediate safety risk
  • Attempting to stall, delay, or disrupt active engagement
  • Waiting for teammates to arrive or regroup
  • Any use of Combat Halt for competitive advantage rather than safety

SECTION 4:

Violations and Penalties

4.1. Penalty for Rule Violations

  • Any player in violation of these rules will receive ice water applied to them at the opening ceremony of the next year and will be disqualified for 6 hours.
  •  If a player commits an infraction, any points gained from that violation will be stripped. As well as any additional point reduction penalties the judge(s) deem necessary.

4.2 Property Damage Guidelines

  • All property damage to a player or non-player is at the cost of the violator.

4.3 Victim Recovery

  • If the violating player eliminated another player while in breach of a rule:
  • The eliminated player is reinstated.
  • The reinstated player receives either a three-hour immunity or immunity until the end of the current day—whichever comes first. (See section 3.2.1 for your required behavior)

SECTION 5:

Oversight and Discretion

5.1. Judge(s) and Rulings

  • For clarification or disputes regarding the rules, contact the designated judge(s). (See homepage)

5.2. Discretionary Authority

  • Judge(s) reserve the right to override, adjust, or interpret rules in any circumstances, especially in the event of a discovered loophole; however, judge(s) will not utilize these powers to manipulate the game with bias.

5.3. Recording Eliminations

  • Recording eliminations while they are happening is encouraged for the purpose of ensuring fair play, but is not mandatory.

  • Video confession from the dead

SECTION 6:

Weapon Definition, Valid Use & Hit Requirements

6.1 Weapon Definition & Valid Use

For a weapon to be considered valid in Water Wars, the water must be released from the “nozzle” or device while under the direct control of the player. This ensures fairness, accountability, and adherence to the spirit of the game.

  • Direct Control means the player must be physically initiating and positioning the water release at the time of the attack. Simply aiming or positioning a device is not sufficient — the player must be actively engaging the trigger, valve, or launch mechanism during use.
  • Examples of Valid Use:
  • Holding and squeezing a water gun or trigger.
  • Throwing a water balloon.
  • Holding and spraying a hose.
  • Examples of Invalid Use:
  • Leaving a hose on the ground and activating it remotely.
  • Rigging a trap to spray water automatically without being present.

This rule implies that water balloons are allowed and count as valid weapons, as long as they are thrown directly by the player. Any use of water-dispersing devices that are automated, delayed, or otherwise not directly triggered by the attacker at the time of impact will not count for elimination. Water balloons must not cause physical damage, as outlined in Section 3.4 Weapon Safety

6.2 Minimum Hit Requirement

  • For a hit to count as a valid elimination, the visible watermark on the target’s clothing or body must be at least the size of a standard U.S. penny (0.75 inches / 19.05 mm in diameter).
  • Any mark smaller than this threshold will not count as a valid hit.
  • The mark must be clearly visible and distinguishable from prior moisture (e.g., sweat, previous hits, environmental water).
  • In cases of dispute, photo or video evidence may be used, and the judge(s) will make the final determination.
